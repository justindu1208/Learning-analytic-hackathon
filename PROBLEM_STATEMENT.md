Learning analytics is about understanding how students learn by looking at data from systems like digital education or assessment platforms. Every time you watch a lecture, take a quiz, or post in a forum, that leaves a (digital) trace.  

Learning analytics turns those traces into insight, not to judge or rank you, but to better understand how students engage, what works, and where support might be needed. It's like feedback, not just for you, but to improve teaching, learning, and the overall student experience.  

It helps us answer questions like:
- Which resources do students keep coming back to?  
- What patterns of activity might show someone needs support?  
- How can teaching teams tailor their approach to diverse learning styles?  

In this hackathon, you are invited to explore digital education platform data and develop analyses, models, or solutions that directly benefit students like yourself or empower staff members to better understand learners. By doing so, you will help amplify the student voice and shape how learning analytics is designed and used across Imperial.  

This is your chance to combine creativity, technical skills, and lived experience to co-create meaningful, student-centred innovation. The data is real, the challenges are real, and the potential impact is huge.

**Problem:** I want a tool/dashboard to monitor my own online learning behaviour and alert me to changes in my usual engagement patterns, and optionally compare with my cohort.

**Problem:** Which videos, readings, or quizzes are interacted with by the majority of students in a cohort, and is their use related to end-of-module marks?

**Impact:** Highlights to teaching staff which resources are most useful and whether interactions with them are linked to success.
